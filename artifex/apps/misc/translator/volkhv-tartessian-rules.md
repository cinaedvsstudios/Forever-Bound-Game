# Volkhv–Tartessian Ritual Dialect

## Canon Grammar and Construction Rules

### 1. Purpose and Scope

The Volkhv–Tartessian Ritual Dialect is Nyx’s family magical language. It belongs to Nyx and her parents’ ritual tradition and must not be confused with Mel’s mother’s Gaulish / Runispeleus scroll system.

The dialect is used for:

- spoken spells
- whispered curses
- binding phrases
- warding notes
- short written warnings
- ritual labels
- inscriptions on ritual tools, vessels and objects

It is not a full conversational language. It is not normally used for ordinary dialogue, letters, long prophecy passages or complete natural speech.

When an English input is too long or conversational, it must be reduced to the shortest ritual construction that preserves the magical intention.

---

## 2. Historical and In-World Status

The dialect combines two fictional inherited layers:

| Layer | Character | Primary Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ancestral Tartessian / Southwestern layer | flowing, vowel-heavy, inscriptional, preserved through family tradition | ordinary ritual vocabulary, ancestral roots, written structure |
| Paternal Volkhv layer | harsh, compressed, consonant-heavy, intrusive | magic-case words, blood magic, possession, darkness, sacrifice, force |

The written glyph system is historically inspired by Southwestern / Tartessian-style signs, but the magical meanings and grammatical system are fictional to the *Forever Bound* universe.

Never claim that a magical meaning is a proven historical Tartessian translation.

Every root must be classified as one of the following:

| Origin Status | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Attested ancestral form** | A form inspired by or preserved from surviving Southwestern / Tartessian material, with a magical meaning assigned by Nyx’s family tradition. |
| **Historically inspired adopted form** | A form taken or adapted from Celtiberian or broader Continental Celtic material and adopted into the family ritual vocabulary. |
| **Invented Volkhv root** | A fixed fictional paternal magical root belonging to the approved family vocabulary. |
| **Proposed new ritual root** | A newly required fictional root not yet approved as canon. |

---

## 3. Core Principle: Ritual Function Matters More Than Ordinary Grammar

The dialect does not attempt to reproduce a complete everyday language. Words are selected and arranged according to their role inside the ritual:

| Ritual Role | Function |
| --- | --- |
| Subject / target | The single thing directly affected, invoked, altered, transformed, commanded or charged by the magic. |
| Action | What is being done to the ritual subject. |
| Descriptor | A quality attached to a noun. |
| Tether / possessor | The source, owner, lineage or thing to which another noun belongs. |
| Spatial / relational marker | The position, origin, direction, accompaniment or temporal/local relation attached to an anchor concept. |
| Adjunct | A supporting ritual role, vessel, instrument, container or assigned function involved in the spell but not directly transformed. |
| Name / place | A named person or named location, always specially enclosed in writing. |
| Negated element | The particular action, state, object or result being forbidden or refused. |

## 5. Vocabulary Source Hierarchy

| Situation | Source Rule | Status Given to New Root |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Concept already has an approved canon root | Use the approved root exactly | Approved canon root |
| Ordinary noun, object, material, role, descriptor, relation or non-magic-case action | Choose the shortest suitable Celtiberian or broader Continental Celtic-derived form | Proposed historically inspired adopted form — awaiting approval |
| A generic concept placed in the magic case | Choose a Proto-Slavic or Old East Slavic source form and adapt it phonetically into the available script sound inventory | Proposed new ritual root — Volkhv layer, awaiting approval |
| A proper name placed as the ritual target | Preserve the name itself; do not replace it with a Slavic-derived word | Proper-name form; enclosed, not translated |

A word does not become Volkhv merely because it appears inside a spell. Only the one concept specifically placed in the magic case follows the Proto-Slavic / Old East Slavic sourcing rule, unless an approved root already exists for that concept.

## 6. Basic Ritual Word Order

Default construction:

**ritual subject or target + action + adjuncts or attached relations**

| Phrase Type | Construction Pattern |
| --- | --- |
| Manipulation spell | affected force or target + action |
| Binding spell | affected force or target + binding/stillness action |
| Vision formula | faculty or target of sight + conduit or enclosed adjunct |
| Ritual warning | dangerous force or target + forbidden condition/action |
| Object label | ordinary noun + descriptor or function |
| Spatial / relational note | anchor noun + attached relation marker |
| Tether phrase | possessed thing + possessor/source |

| English Intention | Construction |
| --- | --- |
| Drain darkness. | `Chrn baare.` |
| Bind blood. | `Khro naŕkee.` |
| Sight through silver. | `Okoo arganto.` |

Separate ritual units are separate words in the romanised ritual output and are divided by the break/seal glyph in the rendered inscription. Compounds formed by description, tethering or attached relations remain joined, unless a proper name interrupts the compound through its enclosure.

---

## 7. Neutral / Object Form

The neutral form is the default form for ordinary ritual vocabulary. Use it for objects, substances, body parts, materials, roles, locations, ordinary actions, ritual tools not being directly transformed, containers not being directly transformed, descriptors not placed in magic case and adjuncts before their visual enclosure is applied.

| If the concept has… | Use… |
| --- | --- |
| An approved canon root | The approved root |
| No approved canon root | The shortest suitable Celtiberian / broader Continental Celtic-derived form |

A mirror used as a container, a ward assigned as a purpose and a gate described in a warning remain neutral unless one is the direct magical target. Silver used as material or conduit uses approved `arganto`.

---

## 8. Magic Case

### 8.1 Definition

The magic case marks the **single principal ritual subject or target**: the thing directly affected, invoked, transformed, awakened, commanded, charged, cursed, bound or otherwise acted upon by magic.

Only the principal ritual subject enters the magic case. The action word, tool, container, conduit, location, assigned role, referenced person and other nouns in the spell do not automatically enter the magic case.

### 8.2 Magic-Case Source Rule

| Situation | Magic-Case Form |
| --- | --- |
| Ritual subject already has an approved canon ritual root | Use the approved root exactly |
| Ritual subject is a generic concept without an approved root | Create a Proto-Slavic or Old East Slavic-derived Volkhv form, phonetically adapted to the glyph system |
| Ritual subject is a named person or place | Preserve the proper name and enclose it with seals; do not invent a replacement magic root |

| English Spell Intention | Ritual Subject in Magic Case | Other Element |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Awaken the stone as a ward. | stone only | ward is neutral and enclosed as an adjunct |
| Bind the spirit in the mirror. | spirit only | mirror is neutral and enclosed as an adjunct |
| Drain the darkness. | darkness: approved `Chrn` | drain: approved `baare` |
| Bind the blood. | blood: approved `Khro` | bind: approved `naŕkee` |
| Curse Nyx through blood. | sealed proper name `Nyx` | blood is neutral or conduit-like unless blood itself is the target |

If a magically affected subject has an adjective, only the head noun is chosen by the magic-case rule. Its adjective remains a postposed descriptor joined to it as a compound before the action is added.

---

## 9. Descriptive Form

Adjectives follow nouns and are written together as a compound unit.

**Formula:** `head noun + adjective`

| English Structure | Ritual Structure |
| --- | --- |
| dark blood | `blood + dark` |
| silver gate | `gate + silver` |
| dead child | `child + dead` |
| black stone | `stone + black` |

Structural examples with approved roots include `KhroChrn` for shadow-dark blood and `OkooChrn` when darkness describes sight rather than being the affected force.

---

## 10. Possessive / Tether Form

The tether form expresses ownership, origin, bloodline, belonging, descent or magical attachment without a word for “of”. The possessed thing or head noun comes first; the possessor or source comes second; they are joined as one compound.

**Formula:** `possessed thing + possessor/source`

| Construction | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `blood + child` | blood of the child |
| `child + blood` | child of blood / blood-linked child |
| `soul + Luca` | soul of Luca |
| `seal + mother` | seal of the mother |
| `blood + Nyx` | blood of Nyx |

When the possessor is a named person or place, the mandatory name enclosure visually interrupts the compound: Nyx’s blood is rendered structurally as `blood {Nyx}`, Luca’s soul as `soul {Luca}`, and Ostangavia’s gate as `gate {Ostangavia}`. The name enclosure rule overrides uninterrupted compound appearance.

---

## 11. Enclosed Adjunct Form

An adjunct is a ritual-supporting element involved in a spell but not itself the principal magically affected subject. It may be a vessel, container, ritual instrument, assigned role, referenced person, named location, destination or supporting conduit.

An adjunct remains in neutral/object form, but in the written script it is enclosed by the break/seal glyph at both beginning and end, just like a proper name.

**Renderer formula:** `{adjunct}`

| English Spell Intention | Magic-Case Subject | Enclosed Adjunct |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Awaken the stone as a ward. | stone | ward |
| Bind the spirit in the mirror. | spirit | mirror |
| Carry the sight through the silver vessel. | sight / `Okoo` | silver vessel |
| Seal the blood at Ostangavia. | blood / `Khro` | place name `Ostangavia` |

Fixed formula exception: `Okoo arganto.` may mean “sight through silver” without enclosing `arganto`, because silver is part of the fixed two-root vision formula rather than a separately presented vessel.

---

## 12. Name and Place Enclosure

Names of people and places always have the break/seal glyph at both beginning and end. This applies at the start, middle or end of a phrase; inside a tether construction; as the ritual target; as an adjunct; and beside an ordinary word-break or phrase ending.

The renderer uses braces to mark an enclosed unit: `{Nyx}`, `{Ostangavia}`, `{mirroreni}`.

Do not print duplicate seals immediately beside an enclosure seal. A closing enclosure at phrase end also functions as the final sentence seal. Adjacent sealed elements should share a boundary glyph where possible rather than printing redundant doubled seals.

If a named person or place is the direct target of magic, preserve the name rather than creating a translated magic-case replacement.

---

Spatial, relational and short temporal meanings are attached after the noun, object, force or operative element that they qualify. They behave like descriptors and tethers: the anchor comes first and the marker follows as one compound.

Coordinate two parallel elements with enclitic `-cue`, attached directly to the second full coordinated unit.

| English Meaning | Construction Logic |

| silver and mirror | `arganto mirrorcue` |
| blood and black stone | `Khro stoneblackcue` |

`-cue` is intended for compact ritual pairings, not long clauses.

## 14. Pronoun and Deictic Object Suffixes

The dialect uses a small set of direct magical object suffixes for short ritual commands rather than a full pronoun system.

| Meaning | Suffix |
| --- | --- |
| me / myself | `-me` |
| you / yourself | `-tu` |
| this / it / that | `-so` |

These attach directly to the operative word: `baareme` = take me, `baaretu` = carry you, and `naŕkeeso` = bind this.

---

## 15. Negation / Prohibition Form

Negation is used for warnings, prohibitions, refusals, blocked actions and forbidden ritual outcomes. Prefix `wero-` attaches directly to the action, state or element prohibited.

| English Meaning | Construction Logic |
| --- | --- |
| do not open | `weroopen` |
| do not bind | `weronaŕkee` |
| no passage | `weropassage` |
| do not drain the blood | `Khro werobaare.` |
| move the vessel away | `vesselIskwero` |

`wero-` is negation only; West, behind, back, away and apart use `Iskwero`.

---

## 16. Action Roots

Action words are not automatically in the magic case.

| Situation | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Action already has an approved canon root | Use the approved root |
| New ordinary action is needed | Use the shortest suitable Celtiberian / broader Continental Celtic-derived form |
| Future canon declares an action a Volkhv force-root | Use that Volkhv form only after formal approval |

| Root | Function |
| --- | --- |
| `baare` | take / carry / draw away / drain |
| `naŕkee` | still / bind / bury / stop / death-bind |

---

## 17. Intensity and Held-Vowel Casting

A final operative root may be extended when an active spell is being sustained, forced or cast powerfully. This is a performance feature, not a normal grammatical ending.

Use an extended final ending only when an active spell is forceful, its intensity is Powerful or Desperate, prolonged/sustained casting is specified, or the form is already canonical.

| Base Root | Extended Cast Form | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `baare` | `baareeoo` | draining or drawing away with sustained force |

| English Intention | Standard Form | Powerful Held Form |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Drain darkness. | `Chrn baare.` | `Chrn baareeoo.` |

When a new held-vowel form is first created, both its base root and its extended cast form must be recorded.

## 18. Written Script Break / Seal Rule

The script includes a special non-phonetic glyph shaped as an X-like crossing with a small circle around its centre, called the **break / seal glyph**.

| Written Function | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Between separate ritual units | word or root break |
| At the end of a complete phrase | final seal / period |
| Before and after a proper name | name enclosure |
| Before and after a named place | place enclosure |
| Before and after a ritual adjunct | adjunct containment |

The old continuous-writing rule is superseded. Separate ritual units are visibly divided by the break/seal glyph. Compounds made by description, tether or relation remain internally joined unless a name or enclosed adjunct interrupts them.

| Typed Input | Renderer Behaviour |
| --- | --- |
| normal space between ritual units | outputs the break/seal glyph |
| normal period at phrase end | outputs the break/seal glyph |
| compound word with no space | outputs uninterrupted joined glyphs |
| braces around a name or adjunct | outputs an opening and closing break/seal glyph around that unit |

### Spoken Seal-Breath Rule

The break/seal glyph may have a spoken ritual articulation without becoming a lexical word.

| Context | Spoken Behaviour |
| --- | --- |
| silent reading of a static label, note or warning | seal is silent |
| ordinary division in a quietly spoken phrase | brief pause only |
| active deliberate spell casting | may be voiced as a short closed breath, noted as `h` |
| final seal of an active cast | closing breath is normally voiced to shut or release the spell |
| enclosure around a name or adjunct during casting | caster may breathe the seal before and after the enclosed unit |

The spoken `h` is not a root and does not produce an extra `h` glyph in the inscription.

## 19. Script Sound and Rendering Rules

The romanised form remains the authoritative linguistic representation. The glyph renderer converts it into the display inscription.

### 19.1 Glyph Inventory

| Group | Characters / Tokens Covered |
| --- | --- |
| Vowels | `a e i o u` |
| Non-stops | `s ś z h ŕ n m l r` |
| Standalone stop consonants | `k p t` |
| K-series syllables | `ka ke ki ko ku` |
| P-series syllables | `pa pe pi po pu` |
| T-series syllables | `ta te ti to tu` |
| Non-phonetic punctuation | break / seal glyph |

### 19.2 Shared Consonant Values

| Romanised Input | Rendered Glyph Family |
| --- | --- |
| `c`, `k`, `g` | shared K-family |
| `p`, `b`, `v` | shared P-family / voiced-bilabial family |
| `t`, `d` | shared T-family |

These equivalences are part of the renderer layer and do not change the authoritative romanised form.

### 19.3 Renderer Adaptation Rules for Missing Sounds

| Canon Romanised Form | Renderer Spelling |
| --- | --- |
| `wero-` | `uero-` |
| `Iskwero` | `Iskuero` |
| `Nyx` | `Niks` |
| `Ostangavia` | `Ostangabia` |

Renderer rules: `w → u`, `v → b`, `y → i`, and `x → ks`. These renderer spellings are visual-conversion forms only, not replacements for canon ritual forms.

Every translation in the Volkhv–Tartessian Ritual Dialect returns exactly two tables.

### Table 1: Ritual Translation

| Required Column | Content |
| --- | --- |
| English Input | Original English input |
| Phrase Type | Spell, binding, vision, warning, label or note |

| Literal Ritual Meaning | Direct in-world ritual sense |
| Inscription Form | Renderer-ready status or rendered output |

### Table 2: Root Origin Key

| Root Type | Required Canon Status |
| --- | --- |

No alternative translations are supplied unless specifically requested.

## 22. Rules That Must Not Be Broken

1. Do not invent a new word where an approved canon root already expresses the concept.
2. Do not describe fictional magical meanings as proven historical Tartessian translations.
3. Do not treat every word in a spell as magic case.
4. Apply magic case only to the single principal ritual subject or target.
5. Keep tools, containers, roles and ritual vessels neutral unless they themselves become the magical subject.
6. Enclose ritual adjuncts, people’s names and place names with the break/seal glyph.
7. Do not add duplicate break/seal glyphs beside an existing enclosure seal.
8. Place adjectives after nouns and join them into compounds.
9. Form possessives as head noun first, possessor second.
10. Attach spatial, relational and short temporal markers after their anchor concept.
11. Use `Iskwero` for West, behind, back, away and apart.
12. Use `wero-` only as the negative or prohibitive prefix.
13. Use `Aram` for East, front and forward.
14. Use `eni`, `ess`, `com`, `kena` and `nust` as approved relation markers.
15. Use `-cue` as the compact enclitic for “and” by attaching it to the second coordinated element.
16. Use `-me`, `-tu` and `-so` only as limited ritual object/deictic suffixes.
17. Keep new ordinary vocabulary Celtic-derived and new magic-case vocabulary Proto-Slavic / Old East Slavic-derived unless future canon changes this.
18. Preserve the romanised form as the authoritative language form and use the glyph system only as its visual rendering.
19. Keep ritual outputs compact, forceful and suitable for inscriptions or spoken spells rather than full conversation.
20. Do not confuse renderer spellings with canon romanised ritual forms.
